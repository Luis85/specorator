Step skipping — "this is a tiny config change"

Not every feature needs all 11 steps. A one-line config tweak doesn't need a vision statement. A dependency bump doesn't need EARS rules. The plugin must be disciplined without being bureaucratic. This flow shows how skipping works, what it logs, and what it doesn't allow.

decision · OQ-4 resolved
2026-05-01 · accepted
Steps can be skipped with a one-line reason — never silently

Choice: Any step can be marked skipped in frontmatter with a mandatory one-line reason. Skipped steps are visible in the stage rail as hollow diamonds, not greyed-out dots — they exist as deliberate gaps, not absences. Gates still run; skipped steps are exempt from their checklist items but the skip reason is displayed in the gate summary.

Why not skip silently: A silent skip is a hidden assumption. If a feature ships with 3 skipped steps and something breaks, the post-mortem can't distinguish "we thought about it and decided not to" from "we forgot." The one-line reason is the audit trail.

Why not require approval to skip: Solo builders don't need permission from themselves. The friction of requiring approval would cause people to just… not use the plugin for small things. Better to have an honest skip than a shadow feature outside the system.

edge cases

When skipping gets complicated

A
un-skipping
"Actually, I do need this step"
A skipped step can be un-skipped at any time before shipping. The stage rail diamond turns back to a dot. The step's files are created from the template (blank, as if new). The skip reason is moved to a comment in frontmatter — kept for the audit trail, not deleted.
# skip_reason (removed): config change only
status: in-progress
Always reversible. Skip reason archived, not erased.
B
skip a gate step
Can you skip step 4 (the gate step)?
Steps 4 and 8 are gate adjacents — they feed directly into the Define→Build and Build→Ship gates. Skipping them is allowed but triggers an extra warning: "This step's content is checked by the gate. Skipping it means the gate will have fewer checks." The gate item becomes exempt-with-warning, shown in amber.
⚑ gate item exempt — step 4 skipped
Gate adjacent = amber, not blocked. Extra warning. Always passable.
C
all steps skipped
What if someone skips everything?
If more than 8 of 11 steps are marked skipped, the cockpit shows a quiet warning: "This feature has almost no spec. Is that intentional?" Not a block — a reality check. Useful for teams reviewing each other's features.
⚑ 9 of 11 steps skipped — is this intentional?
yes, intentional · no, let me review
Reality check, not a block. Nudge at 8+. Always dismissible with confirmation.
design rationale for this flow

Why we made these choices

choice
No "lite mode" — same scaffold, fewer steps

A separate lite mode would be a second product to maintain. Instead, skipping is a first-class action on the standard 11-step scaffold. Small features are just features with some deliberate gaps.

choice
◇ diamond, not greyed dot

A greyed dot looks like "not started yet." A diamond is a different shape — it signals "this position was considered and left intentionally empty." The visual vocabulary needs to distinguish three states: done, pending, and deliberately absent.

choice
Reason mandatory, approval optional

Solo builders need no approval — only an honest reason. Team builders can layer peer-review on top if they want (house rule). The plugin enforces the reason; teams enforce the review. Right responsibility at the right level.

choice
Gate adapts, doesn't break

A gate that hard-fails on a legitimately skipped step is a gate that gets overridden every time. Instead, skipped-step gate items are marked exempt — still visible, still auditable, but not blocking. The gate earns trust by being reasonable.
